Case Study: The Patient Flow Problem

A Solution for the COO

The Problem: The ICU managers lack a single, real time tool to manage patient flow, leading to congestion in their units, and high operational costs.

My Solution: I built a live-connected Power BI dashboard on the 1M+ row MIMIC-III database from MIT. I wrote a single master query to join 5 tables, creating a single, clean fact table, and modeled the data in Power BI. The dashboard tracks 4 critical KPIs: ALOS, Mortality, ER Wait times, and Patient Volume.

  • Stakeholders: COO, ICU managers

After crossing filtering the dashboard for Sepsis patients, I've identified that severe Sepsis patients in the MICU have a 52.38% mortailty rate.

Case Study: The Revenue Problem

A solution for the CFO

The Problem: The hospital finance department is bleeding money from complex claim denials but can't see why. They need a tool to find and recover this lost revenue.

My Solution: I built a 2-page Power BI dashboard on a real-world claims dataset. I used Power query to clean and transform messy billing codes, then wrote 7 DAX measures to track 'Total Revenue Lost' and 'Denial Rate'. The dashboard identifies which denial reasons and which internal teams are the most costly.

  • Clients: CFO, Finance Department
  • Category: Revenue Solutions

Ad-Hoc Scenario

We want to know more about our oldest debt. Which internal team is responsible for the most 451+ day old claims, and which team lost us the most money and what is their #1 denial reason?

I used cross-page filtering to drill deeper into the 451+ day old debt. I discovered that The billing team is responsible for 40% of the 451+ day old claims, and its the AR Team that has lost us the most cash within that aging bucket, contributing to 30% of the loss. Their #1 reason for denial is 'Claim in process', being $13.3k.

Case Study: CHF 30-Day Readmission Analysis

A solution for CMO

The Problem: A hospitals quality team saw CHF readmissions spike but had no idea why. They were treating all patients the same and needed to find the highest-risk group.

My Solution: I used SQL to extract a cohort of all CHF patients from the MIMIC-III database. Then, in a Python Jupyter Notebook, I used Pandas and seaborn to conduct a root cause analysis. I investigated if creatinine was a key factor and disproved the hypothesis, finding that there was no significant difference in creatinine levels between the two groups.

  • Client: CMO
  • Category: Root Cause Analysis

Ad-hoc Scenario

I have a hypothesis that its only the male patients getting readmitted and have high creatinine levels, can you check to see?

I discovered that the males in fact a greater average in creatinine levels than women in this dataset.
